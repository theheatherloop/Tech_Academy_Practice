Savannah cat

The Savannah is a hybrid cat breed. It is a cross between a serval and a domestic cat.

 History

The Savannah cat is the largest of the domestic cat breeds, and grow to be about the size of a medium dog.
On April 7, 1986, Judee Frank crossbred a male serval, belonging to Suzi Woods, with a Siamese domestic cat to produce the first Savannah cat (named Savannah).
In 2001 The International Cat Association©(TICA©) accepted the Savannah as a new registered breed. In May 2012, TICA© accepted it as a championship breed.

 Defining Features

The cats are known for their loyalty, and they will follow their owners around the house. They can also be trained to walk on a leash and to fetch.
An often-noted trait of the Savannah is its jumping ability, which complements its natural tendency to seek out high places. Some Savannahs can leap about
8 feet (2.5 m) high from a standing position. Many Savannah cats do not fear water, and will play or even immerse themselves in water.
